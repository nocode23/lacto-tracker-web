How it works

Designed for the moments when your hands are full

01 — Home

Everything at a glance

The home screen gives you an instant overview of your baby's feeding day. See feeding count, total time, average duration, and your longest session — all without opening anything else.

Quick Overview Last 24 hours

02 — Record

Start a session in one tap

Choose left or right breast, hit the button — a live timer starts immediately. Stop when you're done. Your most recent sessions appear right below so you always know which side you left off on.

Left breast Right breast Live timer

03 — Statistics

Understand your patterns

Flip between 24h, 7-day, 30-day, and all-time views. A trend bar chart reveals rhythm over time, while summary numbers and the full session list let you dig into every detail.

24h · 7d · 30d · All Trend chart

04 — Information

Help when you need it

A built-in how-to guide and FAQ cover everything: the 5-minute rule, how Live Activity works on the Lock Screen, how data is stored, and how to switch the app language instantly.

Built with care

Simple by design, not by accident

🤱

No setup needed

Open the app and start. No account creation, no permissions to grant. Your data lives entirely on your device.

⚡

One-handed operation

Large tap targets and a minimal interface mean you can log a session with your free thumb while holding your baby with the other arm.

📊

Instant statistics

Stats are always current — no syncing, no refresh button. Switch between 24h, 7-day, and 30-day views to spot patterns as they emerge.

🔒

Private by default

No cloud, no analytics on your feeding data, no partner mode to configure. What happens between you and your baby stays on your iPhone.

🌍

Czech & English

Switch the app language instantly from the Information screen. The full interface — including the built-in guide and FAQ — adapts immediately.

🔴

Live Activity

While a session is running, a Live Activity on the Lock Screen and Dynamic Island shows you the elapsed time — no need to unlock your phone.
